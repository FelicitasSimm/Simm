The Videographer Today

What it Takes to be a Videographer

Today's audience absorb information, whether it be for entertainment, learning or advertising through the world of videos. Videos tell a story, at the same time connecting and captivating the audience, with the likes many.

The requirements to be able to paint a visual narrative takes the following:

  • Plenty of planning
  • Hard work
  • Experimentation
  • Collaboration
  • Camera equipment and accessories

It seems that anyone can be a videographer with all the advances in technology plus having tick or say yes to the rest. But allow me to keep writing.

Starting from Scratch

For a beginner, which one comes first. Hard work tops the list. It is different from an established videographer. Planning is their first agenda they as they navigate the world of award recognition within the industry. Blood and sweat. You might well say as videographers of calibre or novice, still has to go through human perseverance.

Recognition only comes after the substantial rolls of video works had come through working with known artists or corporate brand names. Getting involved with Anna Blatman, a local artist here in Australia, gives someone a leap as she goes through her painting of so called ‘No technique’. She came to admit that her painting is of nothing perfect yet the outcome is truly amazing.

Videography is similar to Anna’s world of ‘No Technique’ style of painting. (Click here) Visit Anna Blatman's flatform. Experimentation comes as part of the trade. Having a keen eye to be able to overcome the shortfall caused by either the camera equipment or the man behind the shots as reflected on the footages taken.

The colour between the many shoots don’t match. Of course, this would happen as shoots take place in many locations on different weather conditions. Also, there is the time of the day to take into consideration when the lenses started to roll.

The most important factor is human effort. The camera man’s ability to take advantage of the location and his equipment are within his control on most occasions. Yet at the end of the day, the end result is out of his control. He could only do his best while shooting the actors at play.

The hours of watching video footages can be fun. At times, it could be frustrating as the elements need to shine out of dull settings. That is the main job of a videographer. Make a video leap and grab on to the audience. Sneak into their bedroom and accompany them into their slumbered sleep.

In honesty, planning and hard work comes hand in hand. To see vividly the end product do require forethought and working out how the video is to presented to the public. Hours or replays comes as the videographer need lateral thinking of which way the videotapes can be shorten as to the requirements of the clients.

Common to the industry is that the videographer does not work alone. At times, he is just commissioned to come up with the finished video. There is the director who overlooks the entire project and does the video shooting himself. Or at times, there is the outsourcing for a camera man.

Camera equipment whether owned or not should meet the standard requirement of the output video. They don’t come cheap though running in tens of thousands or topping the hundreds of thousands.

If you are interested in videography, then you soon find out the cost of video equipment and accessories. Pricing has come down though with camera brands outdoing or perhaps the word is outperforming one another trying to capture the market of downsized cameras suitable for commercial application.
